2 Introduction

The website that will be evaluated is that of a pharmacy at the main train station in Graz. It provides an overview of all the services and also includes an online store. Additionally the website also provides the user with news about the pharmacy and general ways to improve health. It is important that the website will be evaluated because people need to have an easy and well- built webpage when they have concerns about their health.

3 Evaluation Procedure

This section describes the procedure used in the heuristic evaluation.

3.1 Evaluation Methodology

The heuristic evaluation is a process which experts utilize to determine the usability of a user interface by conducting independent walkthroughs and by reporting issues that came up. The insights gathered by the evaluators are then used to enhance the usability of the user interface of a product, e.g., a website.

In 1990 two pioneers in web usability, Jakob Nielsen and Rolf Milch, published an article containing a set of heuristics. This article outlines the ten heuristics which a system should include in order to provide the best experience for the user. These heuristics include concepts such as informing users regarding the website's status, employing consistency throughout the site, error prevention, and information visibility to name a few [IDFn.d.].

For our heuristic evaluation we will be using a revised version of Nielsen's usability heuristics called the "Andrews General Usability Heuristics", as shown in Appendix A.1.

Below are the steps we will follow to in order to conduct a Heuristic Evaluation as mentioned in [Nie1994]:

  1. Plan the evaluation: Determine the site that should be tested as well its specific functionality. Include the heuristics that the evaluators will be using while performing a walkthrough of the site.
  2. Conduct the evaluation: These evalutations should be done individually by a group of 5-8 participants. Each individual will review the website while keeping the heuristics in mind, in order to determine both negative and positive findings.
  3. Analyse the results: When the evaluation is finished the information that the individual evaluations produced will be combined and sorted by the evaluators, as a group, based on the severity of the findings.

Even though heuristic evaluation is an extremely powerful tool to enhance the usability of a system, when not done by experts, it is no substitute for usability testing [And2023]. In the end, the evaluation is subjective, and the evaluators are influenced by biases which might distort the results.

3.2 User Profiles

As this website is an online pharmacy which includes an online store as well as general information regarding the pharmacy, the biggest target demographic will be tech savvy people who prefer to buy their medications and other health products online. Additionally, there could be a small group of users who are unable to leave the house and have to order things online. If this person is not particularly tech savvy, they might encounter problems while attempting to order their medications.

This allows us to group users into the following categories:

  1. People who want to live a more balanced and healty lifestyle
    • These people would go onto the website and read up on the general fitness tips under the News section.
  2. People who want to pick up their prescriptions
    • After finding the nearest pharmacy and being guided to this site, the person would then go to the location and pick up their medication.
  3. People who have some hinderance that makes it difficult to go to the pharmacy in person
    • These people are able to navigate to the shop section of the website and order their medication to be delivered.
  4. People who are seeking alternative/natural treatments
    • These people would be able to look on the website to see which herbal and homeopathic remedies are availbe in stores.

4.3 Top Five Problems

The top five problems according to their average (mean) severity ratings are described in more detail below. Problem number 1 is the problem (negative finding) with the highest mean severity. The severity rating scheme used to rank the problems is shown in Table 5.

N01. Links sometimes don't work

Title: Links sometimes don't work
Description: There are some links throughout the website which do not work correctly and leads to a 404 page
Video Clip(s) n01-ru-neg05-site.mp4, n01-md-neg09-not-existing-sites.mp4
Heuristic: A05 Error Prevention
Only When:
Location (How Reproducible?): Homepage -> Scroll down -> Zum Shop(Hyaleron Tagescreme)
Mean Severity: 3.50

There are some links throughout the website which do not work correctly and leads to a 404 page. When on the homepage and clicking "go to shop" on the "Hyaluron Tagescreme", the website displays an error with the message that the site couldn't be found, as shown in Figure 4.

It is important to ensure that all the links on the website work. Therefore it is necessary to check that all links on the website are up to date. It is good however that the website has a custom 404 page.

N02. Issues navigating through website

Title: Issues navigating through website
Description: The website is difficult to navigate
Video Clip(s) n02-vk-neg07-wishlist-nav.mp4
Heuristic: A03 Reversible Actions, A07 Flexibility and Efficiency of Use
Only When:
Location (How Reproducible?): Homepage → Angebote → click product → breadcrumb click "Angebote und Aktionen"
Mean Severity: 3.25

The website is difficult to navigate. There are many parts of the website where the user is unable to navigate to without going back to the home page. The navigation to logically move from one page to another is also missing on many pages. For example, there is an offer section where products are listed with discounts, but when the user clicks on one such offer, there is just a short description but no link or button to add the product to the cart, as shown in Figure 6.

Navigation is an important part of the having a good experience for users. It is therefore important to make sure the navigation is intuitive and that you are always able to navigate well from one page to the next.

N03. Different Navigation Bars

Title: Different Navigation Bars
Description: There are different navigation bars for different parts of the website
Video Clip(s) n03-ru-neg06-navbar.mp4
Heuristic: A04 Consistency, A06 Recognition Rather Than Recall
Only When:
Location (How Reproducible?): Main site -> Sidebar; Shop -> Sidebar
Mean Severity: 3.00

There are different navigation bars for different parts of the website. For example the homepage and the shop navigation bars differ. The sidebar menu for this site changes depending on the page that the user is on, as shown in Figure 7. This leads to the user having difficulties navigating through the website and being frusturated because they are unable to quickly find the page that they want.

It is important to keep consistency throughout the website and therefore the have the navigation bar be the same no matter what page you are on.

N04. Pop-up buttons blocking information

Title: Pop-up buttons blocking information
Description: Throughout the website there are buttons and pop-ups that block information.
Video Clip(s) n04-ks-neg07-fixed-place-for-buttons.mp4
Heuristic: A08 Aesthetic and Minimalist Design, A10 Help and Documentation
Only When:
Location (How Reproducible?): Homepage → Angebote → left arrow
Mean Severity: 2.75

Throughout the website there are buttons and pop-ups that block information. For example the "Frage zum Produkt"-button blocks parts of the product description, as shown in Figure 8. This can lead to the user being frusturated because they are unable to see some important text.

It is therefore necessary to ensure that there is no text or information being covered throughout the website.
