1 Introduction

The website that will be evaluated is that of a pharmacy at the main train station in Graz. It provides an overview of all the services and also includes an online store. Additionally the website also provides the user with news about the pharmacy and general ways to improve health. It is important that the website will be evaluated because people need to have an easy and well- built webpage when they have concerns about their health.

2 Test Methodology

{Describe what a TA Test is and how it is done. Write between 300 and 500 of your own words. Replace my sample text below with your own.}

The Thinking Aloud Test ist used to understand the user experience of a product, for example to detect problems concerning the product or to point out content that should be improved. Thereby test users verbalise what they are doing and think while they perfom several tasks on the product [Off2021]. The test can take place in several forms. However, the basic structure often remains the same. The subject is with a member of the research team in an examination room in which microphones and video cameras are installed to record the session. After receiving all the instructions and answering all the questions, the participant is asked to deal with the task at hand (e.g. reading a text, sifting through the computer program) and saying his or her thoughts aloud. This verbalization is noted in writing by the employee present. In order to take all factors into account in the later analysis, the notes, audio recordings and video materials are combined and analyzed as a whole [Jas2019].

3 User Profiles

As this website is an online pharmacy which includes an online store as well as general information regarding the pharmacy, the biggest target demographic will be tech savvy people who prefer to buy their medications and other health products online. Additionally, there could be a small group of users who are unable to leave the house and have to order things online. If this person is not particularly tech savvy, they might encounter problems while attempting to order their medications.

This allows us to group users into the following categories:

  1. People who want to live a more balanced and healty lifestyle
    • These people would go onto the website and read up on the general fitness tips under the News section.
  2. People who want to pick up their prescriptions
    • After finding the nearest pharmacy and being guided to this site, the person would then go to the location and pick up their medication.
  3. People who have some hinderance that makes it difficult to go to the pharmacy in person
    • These people are able to navigate to the shop section of the website and order their medication to be delivered.
  4. People who are seeking alternative/natural treatments
    • These people would be able to look on the website to see which herbal and homeopathic remedies are availbe in stores.

9 Interview Questions

The following questions will be asked of each user immediately after the final task.

  • Opening Question
    • "How was it?" ["Wie war's?"]
  • Standard Questions
    1. "Did anything strike you as particularly good?"
      ["Gab es etwas, dass besonders gut oder positiv aufgefallen ist?"]
    2. "Did anything strike you as particularly bad?"
      ["Gab es etwas, dass besonders schlecht war?"]
    3. "Did you notice any inconsistencies in the website that made it difficult to navigate?"
      ["Sind Ihnen Ungereimtheiten auf der Website aufgefallen, die die Navigation erschwerten?"]
    4. "How difficult did you find it to complete the tasks and why?"
      ["Wie schwierig fanden Sie es, die Aufgaben auszuführen und warum?"]
  • Individual Questions

    After the standard questions are asked, the interviewer will now ask any questions that arose during the test for each individual user. These questions will correspond to the probe marks in the log.

10 Data Collection and Collation TODO

In order to be able to distinguish the users, a TPid and first name alias will be used when collecting the demographic, behavioural, and attitudinal data for each individual.

During the user testing the exact time to mark the start and end times of each task will be noted.

The data and results from the tests will be collated in the spreadsheet shown in Appendix A.7. This will include the data from the background questionnaires, task success measures, observed problems, ratings from thefeedback questionnaires, etc.

The personal information and data for each test person, including their name, signature, and full video recordings, will be deleted after one year.

Any of the faces that are recorded in the video clips that are then used in the report to illustrate the findings will be blurred or pixelated.
