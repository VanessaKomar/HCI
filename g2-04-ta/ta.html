2 Introduction

The website that will be evaluated is that of a pharmacy at the main train station in Graz. It provides an overview of all the services and also includes an online store. Additionally the website also provides the user with news about the pharmacy and general ways to improve health. It is important that the website will be evaluated because people need to have an easy and well- built webpage when they have concerns about their health.

3 Test Procedure

3.1 Test Methodology

The Thinking Aloud Test is used to understand the user experience of a product, for example to detect problems concerning the product or to point out content that should be improved. Thereby test users verbalise what they are doing and think while they perform several tasks on the product [Off2021]. The test can take place in several forms. However, the basic structure often remains the same. The subject is with the research team in an examination room in which microphones and video cameras are installed to record the session. After receiving all the instructions and answering all of the preliminary questions, the participant is asked to complete the tasks. These tasks, such as finding a specific product on the website, will have the user explore the website. While working through these tasks, the users will say their thoughts aloud. In order to take all factors into account in the later analysis, the notes, audio recordings and video materials are combined and analyzed as a whole [Jas2019]. In order to conduct the Thinking Aloud Test, each group member will have a role in which one is the Facilitator (Administrator, Moderator, Manager, Monitor), the Data Logger (Scribe), the Video Operator, and the Computer Operator [And2023]. This will ensure that the test runs smoothly.

Below are the steps we will follow in order to conduct a Thinking Aloud Test as mentioned in [And2023]:

  1. Develop the Test Plan: This includes the Purpose, Problem Statement, User Profile, Method, Task List, Test Environment, Data to be Collected, and Content of Report
  2. Select and Recruit Participants: User groups consist of users divided based on their characteristics and needs. These groups will be tested separately.
  3. Prepare Test Materials: All of the materials needed for the test need to be prepared prior to the test. This includes the scripts that will be said to the users, the forms that the users will fill out, and the pre planned questions that will be asked of each user.
  4. Run a Pilot Test: A pilot test must be performed going through the entire test procedure. This ensures that any problems that occur can be fixed prior to conducting the actual tests.
  5. Conduct the Real Test: The test will be conducted for each participant and will be recorded and logged. After the tasks are completed, each participant will be debriefed and will fill out a questionnaire.
  6. Analysis and Final Report: After all of the tests are conducted, the data will be analysed. This includes looking at the completion times, error of each user, and other information. A final report will also be written to summarize the findings.

3.2 User Profiles

As this website is an online pharmacy which includes an online store as well as general information regarding the pharmacy, the biggest target demographic will be tech savvy people who prefer to buy their medications and other health products online. Additionally, there could be a small group of users who are unable to leave the house and have to order things online. If this person is not particularly tech savvy, they might encounter problems while attempting to order their medications.

This allows us to group users into the following categories:

  1. People who want to live a more balanced and healty lifestyle
    • These people would go onto the website and read up on the general fitness tips under the News section.
  2. People who want to pick up their prescriptions
    • After finding the nearest pharmacy and being guided to this site, the person would then go to the location and pick up their medication.
  3. People who have some hinderance that makes it difficult to go to the pharmacy in person
    • These people are able to navigate to the shop section of the website and order their medication to be delivered.
  4. People who are seeking alternative/natural treatments
    • These people would be able to look on the website to see which herbal and homeopathic remedies are availbe in stores.

The user group that we intend to test will be Pharmacy customers.

3.6 Training

Overall therewere no interface training given to the users. All of the users are able to and feel comfortable using a laptop and interacting with a website.

In terms of the thinking aloud test, we explained to the users what they needed to do in order to perform this task. This included explaining the basics of what a thinking aloud test is and what they specifically would be doing. We showed the user the example video of the thinking aloud test provided by Andrew Keith. After this we asked the user if they had any questions and let them know that they could ask questions at any time throughout the test.

3.8 Interview Questions

The following questions were asked of each user immediately after the final task. Transcripts of the interviews can be found in Section 4.8.

  • Opening Question
    • "How was it?" ["Wie war's?"]
  • Standard Questions
    1. "Did anything strike you as particularly good?"
      ["Gab es etwas, dass besonders gut oder positiv aufgefallen ist?"]
    2. "Did anything strike you as particularly bad?"
      ["Gab es etwas, dass besonders schlecht war?"]
    3. "Did you notice any inconsistencies in the website that made it difficult to navigate?"
      ["Sind Ihnen Ungereimtheiten auf der Website aufgefallen, die die Navigation erschwerten?"]
    4. "How difficult did you find it to complete the tasks and why?"
      ["Wie schwierig fanden Sie es, die Aufgaben auszuführen und warum?"]
  • Individual Questions

    After the standard questions are asked, the interviewer will now ask any questions that arose during the test for each individual user. These questions will correspond to the probe marks in the log.

3.10 Data Collection and Collation

In order to be able to distinguish the users, a TPid and first name alias will be used when collecting the demographic, behavioural, and attitudinal data for each individual.

During the user testing the exact time to mark the start and end times of each task will be noted. The start of the task will be once the task is finished being read to the test user. The end of the test will be when the test user states that they are finished and the next task is about to be given to the user or when the amounted time is up.

Data from the background questionnaires, task success measures, observed problems, and ratings from the feedback questionnaires were collated into the data spreadsheet (see Appendix A.8).

The personal information and data for each test person, including their name, signature, and full video recordings, will be deleted after one year.

Any of the faces that were recorded in the video clips that are then used in the report to illustrate the findings were blurred or pixelated.
