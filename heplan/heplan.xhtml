2 Evaluation Methodology

The heuristic evaluation is a process which experts utilize to determine the usability of a user interface by conducting independent walkthroughs and by reporting issues that came up. The insights gathered by the evaluators are then used to enhance the usability of the user interface of a product, e.g., a website.

In 1990 two pioneers in web usability, Jakob Nielsen and Rolf Milch, published an article containing a set of heuristics. This article outlines the ten heuristics which a system should include in order to provide the best experience for the user. These heuristics include concepts such as informing users regarding the website's status, employing consistency throughout the site, error prevention, and information visibility to name a few [IDFn.d.].

For our heuristic evaluation we will be using a revised version of Nielsen's usability heuristics called the "Andrews General Usability Heuristics", as shown in Appendix A.1.

Below are the steps we will follow to in order to conduct a Heuristic Evaluation as mentioned in [Nie1994]:

  1. Plan the evaluation: Determine the site that should be tested as well its specific functionality. Include the heuristics that the evaluators will be using while performing a walkthrough of the site.
  2. Conduct the evaluation: These evalutations should be done individually by a group of 5-8 participants. Each individual will review the website while keeping the heuristics in mind, in order to determine both negative and positive findings.
  3. Analyse the results: When the evaluation is finished the information that the individual evaluations produced will be combined and sorted by the evaluators, as a group, based on the severity of the findings.

Even though heuristic evaluation is an extremely powerful tool to enhance the usability of a system, when not done by experts, it is no substitute for usability testing [And2023]. In the end, the evaluation is subjective, and the evaluators are influenced by biases which might distort the results.

3 User Profiles

As this website is an online pharmacy which includes an online store as well as general information regarding the pharmacy, the biggest target demographic will be tech savvy people who prefer to buy their medications and other health products online. Additionally, there could be a small group of users who are unable to leave the house and have to order things online. If this person is not particularly tech savvy, they might encounter problems while attempting to order their medications.

This allows us to group users into the following categories:

  1. People who want to live a more balanced and healty lifestyle
    • These people would go onto the website and read up on the general fitness tips under the News section.
  2. People who want to pick up their prescriptions
    • After finding the nearest pharmacy and being guided to this site, the person would then go to the location and pick up their medication.
  3. People who have some hinderance that makes it difficult to go to the pharmacy in person
    • These people are able to navigate to the shop section of the website and order their medication to be delivered.
  4. People who are seeking alternative/natural treatments
    • These people would be able to look on the website to see which herbal and homeopathic remedies are availbe in stores.
